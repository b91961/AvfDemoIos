Visit Programmableweb.com and search for JSON-P data sources. Why would we need JSON-P, specifically?

JSON stands for JavaScript Object Notation. JSON-P stands for JSON with Padding. JSON is a lightweight text-based standard designed for human-readable data interchange. Instead of using AJAX, JSONP injects dynamic (script) tags to the document. It is a known fact that we can include scripts from other domains using the script tag. So in turn this allows for cross-domain calls.

Based on the JSON-P search results: what data resource(s) do you think might pair well with which types of Native device features?

JSON-P is primarily used in creating dynamic web site plugins like AdSense and badges, which are used in multiple sites dynamically to provide extensions. Some other data resources that might pair well with Native device features would be: Instagram with the camera, Dropbox with file, Google Maps with Geolocation and Twitter with notifications.

Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

I watched some demo videos on both the screen reader and the mobile screen reader. Neither of them displayed any problem or frustrations with the multiple videos that I watched. From my viewpoint as a developer, the one problem that I could see being a frustration would be the designer not using alt tags. If you have a picture of a Facebook logo with no text for the screen reader to read AB blind person would only know that was Facebook by the screen reader reading the alt tab of the picture.

Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

The two most common type of devices that are made to specifically support accessibility would be a computer and a cell phone. Some features that both of these devices would have from the factory, would be magnifier for the vision impaired, screen readers for the blind, on screen dictation for the deaf And also Braille keyboards for the blind. There are a lot more accessibility features which you could explore by going to the settings on your phone or PCM and selecting accessibility.

Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

LookTel money reader for iOS devices allows a blind person to count money by using the iPhone camera. The app recognizes US currency and the screen reader announces the amount of the bill. Color identifier for iOS uses the iPhone camera to identify colors for the blind. This could be very useful in everyday life while getting dressed or shoppingIt besides those couple uses it is a way for a blind person to be in touch with a sense they never had or lost.

What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.? Japan? Great Britain?

According to statista.com the top 3 Apple App Store categories world wide as of July 2013 were Games at number 1 with 16.98% of the market, Education at number 2 with 10.83% of the market and Entertainment at number 3 with 8.70% of the market (statista.com, 2013).

Above is the only research that I found supporting the actual top grossing categories. Below I will list the top apps in each country as of August 26, 2013.

In the United States listed from highest to lowest would be Asphalt 8: Airborne, Minecraft â€“ Pocket Edition and finally RotoWire Fantasy Football Draft Kit 2013 (148apps.com, 2013). Which the top 2 are in the games category and the third is under the sports category.

In Japan listed from highest to lowest would be Asphalt 8: Airborne, AKB48, and Minecraft â€“ Pocket (topappoftheworld.com, 2013). All 3 of the apps are in the games category.

In Great Britain listed from highest to lowest would be Asphalt 8: Airborne, 7 Minute Workout and Motocross Elite (topappoftheworld.com, 2013). Numbers 1 and 3 are games and number 2is in the Health& Fitness category.

Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

I can infer that globally the games category is where you want to be producing apps for the best gross sales. I think that the games help pass time during micro breaks. This is good research to keep in mind when deciding to develop an app. Also with the same game being top grossing globally the developer should make the game in multiple languages so they can sell the app globally.

What are the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

While developing an app you should always keep in mind data costs. Where data is more expensive the developer should think about using local storage instead of pushing data and give the user the option for manual push. In the 3 markets talked about in this discussion the data rates are as follows.

In postpaid data plans, the U.S. offers $15.00 for 200 megabytes (MB) per month ($0.08/MB), lower than Japan, which is at $4.70 for 0.5MB ($9.40/MB). However, the U.S. rate is more then 2 times more expensive than Great Britain at $7.70 for 5120MB ($0.002/MB) (New America Foundation , 2010).

In the case of unlimited data plans, the U.S. is in the middle tier of service rates. The U.S. offers a plan of $29.99 per month, which is cheaper than Japan ($52.60/month). Great Britain does not offer an unlimited data plan (New America Foundation , 2010).

It is important to note that in most countries, consumers can only subscribe to data plans after they subscribe to voice and text plans. Thus, even though certain countries offer cheaper postpaid or unlimited data plans, they end up paying higher price for a complete cell phone package if voice and text rates are higher (New America Foundation , 2010).

And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.

My personal opinion would be a productivity app. I very rarely play games on my mobile phone. From the research that was done for this discussion from a project standpoint I would now want to develop a game. The game would most likely be a racing game or RPG game as statistics show them to be the top grossing. I would also want to develop the games being multi lingual to be able to market it globally. Also depending on the market I would create different options for manual or automatically syncing the info to the server.
